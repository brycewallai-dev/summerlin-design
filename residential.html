The approach

Good residential design starts with how you actually live.

A lot of residential design looks great in a photograph and doesn't hold up on a Tuesday morning. Furniture that collects clutter, lighting that doesn't work for how a family actually uses a room, layouts that make sense in a floor plan and not in real life. The goal here is different: spaces that work before they look beautiful, and do both.

Every project — no matter the size — starts with understanding how the space is actually going to be used. That informs every selection, every layout decision, every material choice. It's what 250 projects of doing this work builds.

How it works

A clear process. Your home, not a template.

Every project follows the same five steps — so you always know where things stand and what comes next.

01

Initial Consultation

A conversation about your home, your goals, and how you actually live in the space. We'll talk through what's working, what isn't, and what a realistic project looks like. No obligation.

02

Proposal & Scope

A written proposal with defined scope, timeline, and fee structure before any design work begins. You know your total investment before we start — no surprises mid-project.

03

Concept & Selections

Design concepts, material palettes, and space plans built around your home specifically. You'll see the direction and approve it before anything is ordered or committed.

04

Procurement

Every furniture piece, fixture, and material is sourced and managed — vendor coordination, lead times, and deliveries included. One point of contact for everything that comes into your home.

05

Installation & Final Walkthrough

On-site coordination through installation, with a final walkthrough to confirm every detail is right before we call it done.
